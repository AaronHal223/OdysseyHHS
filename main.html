About Our Program

After two decades of attempts by the Hillsborough school community to establish an Odyssey of the Mind program at the high school level, Aaron Haldar, along with Aarav and Arnav Chamunya, successfully founded the Hillsborough High School chapter in 2024. What began as a vision to bring creative problem-solving competitions to HHS has grown into one of the school's most innovative STEM programs.

In our inaugural 2024-2025 season, we:

  • Competed in two competition problems at Regional and State levels
  • Developed engineering solutions that held 310 pounds with limited materials
  • Expanded from 12 founding members to 30 participants in our second year
  • Established partnerships with local businesses and schools

Our Historic Journey

2004

The First Attempts

Hillsborough Middle School begins Odyssey of the Mind program, with hopes to expand to the high school. Despite multiple attempts over 20 years, a sustainable high school program fails to materialize due to lack of student leadership and institutional support.

2023

A Vision Takes Shape

Aaron Haldar, drawing from his middle school Odyssey experience, begins researching what it would take to establish the program at HHS. He recruits Aarav and Arnav Chamunya as co-founders, and together they develop a comprehensive proposal.

2024

Program Approval

After presenting to school administrators and securing faculty sponsorship, the team receives official approval in September 2024. They recruit 12 founding members and begin training for their first competition season.

2025

Competition Success

Against all expectations for a first-year program, the team places 2nd in Regionals for Problem 4 (engineering) and advances to State Finals. Their innovative bridge solution holds 310 pounds, earning recognition from veteran teams.

2025+

Expanding Impact

With membership more than doubling, the team implements outreach programs to local elementary schools, establishes corporate sponsorships, and creates a sustainable leadership structure to ensure the program's future.

Competition Problem Solutions

2nd Place Regionals • State Qualifier

Problem 4: The Structure

Challenge: Create a balsa wood structure with limited weight that can hold maximum pounds during a performance.

Our Solution:

  • Innovative triangular lattice design maximizing strength-to-weight ratio
  • Precision engineering with 0.1mm tolerance on all joints
  • Held 310 pounds at State Finals - outperforming many veteran teams

Key Contributors:

Aaron Haldar developed the initial design concept and engineering calculations. Pranet Godavarty and Arjun Nandurkar led construction, spending over 120 hours perfecting the structure.

Creative Excellence Award

Problem 5: Stellar Stories

Challenge: Create a humorous performance about myths and legends surrounding stars and constellations.

Our Solution:

  • Original myth combining Greek and Hindu astronomy traditions
  • Interactive constellation map created with Arduino and LED lights
  • Handcrafted costumes representing celestial bodies

Key Contributors:

Owen Sparks wrote the script and directed performances. Hania Bilal designed props and costumes. The entire team contributed to developing our unique stellar narrative.
